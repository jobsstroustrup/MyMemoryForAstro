# Jing Li · 李婧

## 中文版

### 基本信息
- **姓名**: Jing Li · 李婧 (Prof. Jing Li)
- **职位**: 北京大学物理学院大气与海洋科学系教授（博雅特聘教授）
- **研究方向**: 大气气溶胶遥感、MODIS + CALIPSO 主被动卫星联合反演、气溶胶辐射强迫与气候效应
- **公开主页**: [PKU 大气与海洋科学系全职教师名录](https://www.atmos.pku.edu.cn/en/Faculty/Fulltimefaculty/index.htm)
- **所属研究组**: PKU 大气化学与气溶胶建模 (ACM) 研究组

### 与 Zhenyu 的合作
- **时间**: 2021 年 12 月 — 2022 年 6 月
- **方向**: 北京大学本科毕业论文研究 — *"Development of an Aerosol Joint Retrieval Algorithm for MODIS and CALIPSO Sensors"*
- **技术内容**: 在李婧老师指导下，完成了 MODIS（被动）+ CALIPSO（主动）卫星迭代联合反演气溶胶光学厚度（AOD）的算法开发，并在 4 个北京案例上验证了精度提升。
- **致谢角色**: 本科毕业论文指导教师，每周工作汇报与逐稿审阅。

### 公开研究成果（节选）
- 长期从事卫星气溶胶遥感、地面光学厚度（AOD）反演、能见度反演等研究。
- 参与 NSFC 项目 *"过去 40 年全球陆地气溶胶直接辐射强迫的观测约束"*。

## English Version

### Basic Info
- **Name**: Jing Li · 李婧 (Prof. Jing Li)
- **Position**: Boya Distinguished Professor, Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences, School of Physics, Peking University
- **Research focus**: Aerosol remote sensing; MODIS + CALIPSO active-passive joint retrieval; aerosol radiative forcing and climate effects
- **Public faculty page**: [PKU Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences — Full-time Faculty](https://www.atmos.pku.edu.cn/en/Faculty/Fulltimefaculty/index.htm)
- **Research group**: PKU Atmospheric Chemistry and Aerosol Modeling (ACM) Group

### Collaboration with Zhenyu
- **Period**: December 2021 — June 2022
- **Topic**: Peking University senior thesis research — *"Development of an Aerosol Joint Retrieval Algorithm for MODIS and CALIPSO Sensors"*
- **Technical scope**: Under Prof. Jing Li's supervision, Zhenyu developed an iterative joint-retrieval algorithm coupling MODIS (passive) and CALIPSO (active) spaceborne sensors for aerosol optical depth (AOD), validated on four Beijing case studies.
- **Acknowledgment role**: Senior thesis advisor; weekly progress reviews and draft-by-draft feedback.

### Selected Public Research
- Long-standing work on satellite aerosol remote sensing, ground-based AOD retrieval, and visibility retrieval.
- NSFC-funded project *"Observational Constraint of Direct Radiative Forcing of Aerosols over Global Land during the Past 40 Years."*
